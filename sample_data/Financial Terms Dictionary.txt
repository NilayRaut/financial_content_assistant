# Financial Terms Dictionary

## Banking Terms

### A-B
- **Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**: The interest rate and fees charged per year for a loan or credit card, expressed as a percentage.
- **Annual Percentage Yield (APY)**: The effective annual rate of return taking into account the compounding of interest on an account.
- **Assets**: Anything of value owned by a person or organization, including cash, investments, and property.
- **Automated Clearing House (ACH)**: A network that processes electronic payments and money transfers between banks.
- **Available Balance**: The amount in an account that can be withdrawn or invested, excluding pending transactions.
- **Balance**: The total amount of funds in your account.
- **Budget**: A plan that outlines expected income and expenses for a specific period.

### C-D
- **Certificate of Deposit (CD)**: A time deposit that offers a fixed interest rate for a specified term.
- **Credit Score**: A numerical expression based on credit history that represents a person's creditworthiness.
- **Debit Card**: A card that allows direct access to a checking account for purchases and ATM withdrawals.
- **Deposit**: An addition to an account, such as cash or checks added to a bank account.
- **Direct Deposit**: Automatic electronic deposit of wages or benefits into a bank account.
- **Diversification**: Spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk.

### E-F
- **Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)**: Moving money between accounts through electronic payment systems.
- **Emergency Fund**: Money set aside for unexpected expenses or financial hardships.
- **FDIC Insurance**: Federal deposit insurance that protects bank deposits up to $250,000 per depositor, per bank.
- **Fixed Rate**: An interest rate that remains constant throughout the loan term.

### I-L
- **Interest**: The cost of borrowing money or the return earned on deposited funds.
- **Liabilities**: Debts or financial obligations owed to others.
- **Liquidity**: How quickly an asset can be converted to cash without significant loss of value.
- **Loan**: Money borrowed that must be repaid, usually with interest.

### M-P
- **Minimum Balance**: The lowest amount required in an account to avoid fees or qualify for certain benefits.
- **Mobile Banking**: Accessing bank accounts through a smartphone app.
- **Overdraft**: When withdrawals exceed available funds in an account.
- **Prime Rate**: The interest rate that banks charge their most creditworthy customers.

### R-Z
- **Routing Number**: A nine-digit code that identifies a financial institution in a transaction.
- **Savings Account**: An interest-bearing deposit account used for storing money.
- **Transaction**: Any activity that affects an account balance, such as deposits, withdrawals, or transfers.
- **Variable Rate**: An interest rate that can change during the loan term.

## Investment Terms

### A-B
- **Alpha**: The excess return of an investment relative to the return of a benchmark index.
- **Asset Allocation**: The distribution of investments among different asset categories to balance risk and reward.
- **Bear Market**: A declining market characterized by falling prices and widespread pessimism.
- **Beta**: A measure of an investment's volatility compared to the market as a whole.
- **Bond**: A debt security where the issuer owes the holder a debt and pays interest and/or principal at a later date.
- **Bull Market**: A rising market characterized by increasing prices and investor optimism.

### C-D
- **Capital Gain**: Profit from selling an investment for more than its purchase price.
- **Compound Interest**: Interest calculated on both the initial principal and accumulated interest.
- **Dividend**: A portion of a company's earnings distributed to shareholders.
- **Diversification**: Spreading investments across various assets to reduce risk.
- **Dollar-Cost Averaging**: Investing a fixed amount regularly regardless of market conditions.

### E-I
- **ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)**: An investment fund traded on stock exchanges like individual stocks.
- **Equity**: Ownership interest in a company, typically in the form of stock.
- **Index Fund**: A passively managed fund designed to track a specific market index.
- **Inflation**: The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises.
- **Interest Rate**: The percentage charged by a lender to a borrower for the use of assets.

### M-P
- **Market Capitalization**: The total value of a company's outstanding shares.
- **Mutual Fund**: An investment vehicle made up of a pool of funds from many investors.
- **Portfolio**: A collection of investments owned by an individual or organization.
- **Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E)**: A valuation ratio of a company's current share price compared to its earnings per share.

### R-Z
- **REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust)**: A company that owns, operates, or finances income-producing real estate.
- **Return on Investment (ROI)**: A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment.
- **Risk Tolerance**: An investor's ability and willingness to endure market volatility.
- **Stock**: A security representing ownership in a corporation.
- **Yield**: The income returned on an investment, such as interest or dividends.

## Economic Terms

### A-F
- **Bonds**: Fixed-income securities issued by governments or corporations to raise money.
- **Consumer Price Index (CPI)**: A measure of the average change over time in prices paid by consumers for goods and services.
- **Fiscal Policy**: Government spending and taxation decisions to influence the economy.
- **Futures**: Contracts to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price at a specific future date.

### G-M
- **GDP (Gross Domestic Product)**: The total value of goods and services produced within a country's borders.
- **Inflation**: The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises.
- **Monetary Policy**: Actions of a central bank to influence the money supply and credit conditions.

### R-Z
- **Recession**: A significant decline in economic activity lasting for at least six months.
- **S&P 500**: A stock market index tracking the performance of 500 large companies listed on U.S. stock exchanges.
- **Treasury Bill**: A short-term debt obligation backed by the U.S. government with a maturity of less than one year.
- **Yield Curve**: A line plotting the interest rates of bonds with equal credit quality but differing maturity dates.
